Price risk

Skin prices can go to zero. A single Facepunch patch can invalidate an entire collection overnight. A new drop can saturate supply and crash the floor. A trading restriction at the Steam level can make an item unsellable. We show price history as a guide — past performance does not predict future results. Never spend more on a skin than you can afford to lose.

Counterparty risk

Every trade on SkinBox is between two Steam users. We facilitate the handoff, but we cannot guarantee the behavior of the person on the other side. Sellers can cancel at the last moment. Buyers can chargeback. Bots can be hacked. Users can be banned by Steam between list and trade. Each of these scenarios is a real-world risk you accept by using the Service.

Technical risk

Software breaks. Networks go down. Databases get corrupted. Third-party APIs have outages. We do our best to keep SkinBox up 24/7 but we do not promise any uptime SLA to end users. Bug-caused losses are not covered unless they fall within the narrow refund cases listed in the Refund Policy.

Regulatory risk

The legal status of in-game item trading is unsettled in many jurisdictions. Regulators have shut down or restricted similar services before, sometimes with no warning. We comply with all applicable law in our operating jurisdiction but cannot guarantee the Service will remain available in yours. If SkinBox is ever forced to shut down, we will attempt to process all pending withdrawals — but we do not guarantee that every user will be made whole.

Custodial risk

Wallet balances on SkinBox are not bank deposits. They are not insured by any government deposit-insurance scheme. They are held by SkinBox's payment processor (Stripe) under Stripe's terms of service, which we do not control. If Stripe freezes the account, your balance may be inaccessible. If Stripe terminates SkinBox, your balance may be delayed in reaching you.

User-error risk

We cannot undo mistakes. If you:

  • Type the wrong price and a buyer snipes it for a dollar — that sale stands.
  • Send items to the wrong Steam account — we can't recover them.
  • Enter the wrong bank details and Stripe bounces the payout — you owe the recovery fee.
  • Get phished outside SkinBox and hand over your Steam Guard code — we cannot recover your inventory.
